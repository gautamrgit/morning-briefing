## 🧘 Philosophy

**On earnings numbers that miss and stocks that rise anyway** — Wipro missed on both revenue and profit yesterday, yet its shares rose almost 2% because deal bookings suggested a credible path forward. It's a useful reminder, one the Stoics would recognise, that markets — like most judgment — respond less to the raw fact of an outcome than to the story it's embedded in about what comes next. For any results-day communication, the lesson isn't to obscure a weak number, but to be unusually clear about what specifically it does and doesn't say about the trajectory ahead.

**On returning to a game that changed while you were away** — Alcaraz's four-month absence means he returns to a tour where the rankings, the rivalries and even the "favourite" for his own title have shifted without him. Marcus Aurelius wrote often about resuming duty after interruption — the discipline isn't in avoiding all disruption, which is rarely possible, but in re-engaging fully with conditions as they now are rather than as they were when you left. Any leader returning from an extended absence — illness, sabbatical, a reassignment — faces some version of the same task: assess the terrain freshly rather than assuming it waited for you.
