## 🧘 Philosophy

**On inflation and the discipline of desire** — Today's CPI print, breaching the RBI's 4% comfort zone for the first time in 17 months, is really a story about scarcity reshaping behaviour: food and fuel costs rising faster than incomes forces households — and the FMCG companies serving them — to relearn restraint. The Epicureans distinguished between natural-necessary desires (food, shelter, basic comfort) and desires inflated by circumstance or status; inflation is, in effect, a forced return to the necessary. For any commercial planning built on discretionary upgrade cycles, this print is a reminder that the next few quarters may reward whoever best serves necessity cheaply, not whoever best sells aspiration.

**On rankings that move without playing a match** — Alcaraz dropped to world No. 3 this week without hitting a single ball at Wimbledon, purely because Zverev and Sinner kept winning matches he couldn't contest. It's a clean illustration of a principle the Stoics returned to often: your position relative to others is never entirely up to you, no matter how excellent your own effort is. What is up to you is preparation and conduct during the periods you can control. The lesson translates directly to competitive strategy — a rival's absence from the field doesn't freeze your own ranking in place; standing still while others move is itself a kind of falling behind.
